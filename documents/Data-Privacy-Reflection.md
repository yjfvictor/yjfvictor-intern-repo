## üîç Research & Learn

### Review Focus Bear's **privacy policy** and note key takeaways

**Source:** [Focus Bear Privacy Policy](https://www.focusbear.io/privacy-policy)

**Key Takeaways:**

1. **Compliance Framework:**
   - Focus Bear complies with the **General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)** (EU) 2016/679
   - The company is the "Controller" responsible for data processing
   - 'by Data' serves as the EU Representative and Data Protection Officer (DPO)
   - Contact for privacy concerns: **privacy@focusbear.io**

2. **Data Processing Principles:**
   - All data is processed **fairly, lawfully, and transparently**
   - Data is only collected for **specific, clear purposes**
   - Data is **not further processed for different purposes** without consent
   - Processing is based on legal grounds: consent, contract execution, legal obligations, or legitimate interest

3. **Security Measures:**
   - **Double encryption** for habit data (so no one else can see it apart from the user)
   - Secure providers for authentication (Auth0, Zoho)
   - Payment processing through trusted providers (Stripe, Paddle, RevenueCat) - card details not stored by Focus Bear
   - **Anonymisation** of survey and analytics data
   - Regular evaluation and audits of security measures
   - Technical and organisational measures to ensure confidentiality, integrity, availability, and resilience

4. **Data Retention:**
   - Data is retained only for the time necessary to fulfill the purpose for which it was collected
   - May be retained longer for statistical purposes with appropriate security measures and anonymisation
   - Users can exercise their **Right to Erasure** to request deletion

5. **User Rights:**
   - Right of Access, Rectification, Erasure, Object, Restriction, Portability
   - Right to withdraw consent
   - Right to lodge complaints with Supervisory Authorities
   - Right not to be subject to automated decision-making

6. **Data Breach Management:**
   - Focus Bear has processes to **evaluate, manage, and report** potential personal data security breaches
   - Breaches must be reported to the Supervisory Authority in accordance with GDPR

### What types of **data** are considered confidential at Focus Bear?

Based on the privacy policy, the following types of data are considered confidential:

1. **Identification Data:**
   - Email addresses
   - Phone numbers
   - Login credentials
   - Authentication information

2. **Social Data:**
   - Personal habits created in the app (e.g., "Do yoga", "Read bible", "Take Dex")
   - Lifestyle information
   - Optional survey responses

3. **Academic & Professional Data:**
   - Occupation information
   - Education and work background

4. **Commercial and Marketing Data:**
   - Email addresses for marketing
   - WhatsApp numbers
   - Marketing preferences

5. **Technical Data:**
   - Operating system and device type
   - Error logs and crash reports
   - Detailed logs (when users report problems)

6. **Economic, Financial and Insurance Data:**
   - Payment card information (handled by third-party processors, not stored by Focus Bear)
   - Subscription and purchase history

7. **Profile Data:**
   - Purchase history
   - Service preferences
   - Feedback and survey responses

8. **Special Category Personal Data** (requires enhanced protection):
   - **Health information:** ADHD diagnosis, autism diagnosis, medication habits (e.g., "Take Dex", "Morning insulin")
   - **Religious beliefs:** Implied through habits (e.g., "Read bible", "Read Quran")

9. **Company Information:**
   - Internal business data
   - Strategic plans
   - Financial information
   - Employee information
   - Customer lists and relationships

**Key Protection Measures:**
- Habit data is **double encrypted** so only the user can see it
- Survey data is kept **anonymous** and not linked to personal details
- Only team members with a **need to know** can access user emails
- Personal device details are **anonymised** and not shared outside Focus Bear

### What are **best practices** for handling confidential data?

**General Best Practices:**

1. **Principle of Least Privilege:**
   - Only access data you need for your specific role
   - Don't access user data unless necessary for your work
   - Use the minimum amount of data required

2. **Secure Storage:**
   - Store sensitive data in secure, encrypted systems
   - Use company-approved storage solutions (not personal devices or cloud accounts)
   - Follow Focus Bear's data storage policies

3. **Secure Transmission:**
   - Use encrypted channels for transmitting sensitive data
   - Avoid sending sensitive data via unencrypted email
   - Use secure file-sharing methods when necessary

4. **Access Controls:**
   - Use strong, unique passwords
   - Enable two-factor authentication where available
   - Don't share login credentials
   - Log out of systems when not in use

5. **Data Minimisation:**
   - Only collect data that is necessary
   - Don't retain data longer than needed
   - Delete or anonymise data when no longer required

6. **Anonymisation and Pseudonymisation:**
   - When possible, work with anonymised or pseudonymised data
   - Remove personally identifiable information when not needed
   - Use aggregate data for analysis when possible

7. **Clear Communication:**
   - Be transparent about what data is collected and why
   - Obtain proper consent before collecting data
   - Clearly explain how data will be used

8. **Regular Audits and Reviews:**
   - Regularly review what data you have access to
   - Remove access when no longer needed
   - Report any suspicious activity

9. **Physical Security:**
   - Lock screens when away from your computer
   - Secure physical documents containing sensitive information
   - Don't leave sensitive information visible in public spaces

10. **Incident Response:**
    - Know who to contact if you suspect a data breach
    - Report incidents immediately
    - Document what happened and what data may have been affected

**Focus Bear-Specific Practices:**

1. **Habit Data:**
   - Never access user habit data unless the user has reported a problem and asked you to look
   - Remember that habit data is double encrypted and should remain private
   - Be aware that habits may reveal sensitive information (health conditions, religious beliefs)

2. **Survey Data:**
   - Work with anonymised aggregate data when possible
   - Don't link survey responses to individual users
   - Respect the sensitive nature of health-related survey data (ADHD, autism diagnoses)

3. **User Communications:**
   - Only team members who need to communicate with users should see email addresses
   - Keep user communications confidential
   - Don't share user information outside Focus Bear

4. **Third-Party Services:**
   - Be aware of which third-party services handle user data
   - Understand the data protection measures of processors (Auth0, AWS, Stripe, etc.)
   - Follow data sharing agreements and policies

### How should you **respond to a suspected data breach** or accidental disclosure of confidential information?

**Immediate Actions (Within Hours):**

1. **Contain the Breach:**
   - If possible, immediately stop or limit the breach
   - Change passwords or revoke access if credentials were compromised
   - Isolate affected systems if applicable
   - Don't try to cover it up or delay reporting

2. **Report Immediately:**
   - Contact **privacy@focusbear.io** immediately
   - Contact your supervisor or manager
   - If urgent, contact the Data Protection Officer (DPO) at **dpo@bydata.eu**
   - Don't wait to gather all information - report first, then provide details

3. **Document the Incident:**
   - Write down what happened, when it happened, and how you discovered it
   - Note what data may have been affected
   - Document any actions you've already taken
   - Keep a record of who you've notified

**Assessment and Response (Within 24-72 Hours):**

4. **Assess the Scope:**
   - Determine what types of data were involved
   - Identify how many users or records may be affected
   - Assess the potential impact and risk
   - Determine if the breach is ongoing or has been contained

5. **Follow Company Procedures:**
   - Focus Bear has processes for "Security Breach Management" to evaluate, manage, and report breaches
   - Work with the privacy team and DPO to assess the breach
   - Follow GDPR requirements for breach notification

6. **Notification Requirements:**
   - Under GDPR, breaches must be reported to the Supervisory Authority within **72 hours** if there is a risk to individuals' rights and freedoms
   - Affected individuals must be notified if there is a high risk to their rights and freedoms
   - The company will handle official notifications, but your prompt reporting is critical

7. **Remediation:**
   - Work with the team to address any vulnerabilities
   - Implement additional security measures if needed
   - Review and improve processes to prevent future breaches

**What NOT to Do:**

- **Don't delay reporting** - time is critical for breach response
- **Don't try to fix it alone** - involve the appropriate team members
- **Don't delete evidence** - preserve information needed for investigation
- **Don't discuss the breach publicly** - let the company handle communications
- **Don't blame others or make excuses** - focus on resolving the issue

**Prevention:**

- Follow security best practices to prevent breaches
- Be cautious when handling sensitive data
- Report suspicious activity immediately
- Stay informed about security threats and updates

### How should you **store, share, and dispose of sensitive information** safely?

**Storage:**

1. **Use Approved Systems:**
   - Store data in company-approved, secure systems
   - Use encrypted storage solutions
   - Follow Focus Bear's data storage policies
   - Don't use personal cloud accounts or devices for company data

2. **Access Controls:**
   - Ensure files are stored with appropriate access controls
   - Use password protection for sensitive documents
   - Don't store sensitive data in publicly accessible locations

3. **Backup and Recovery:**
   - Use secure backup solutions
   - Ensure backups are also encrypted and secure
   - Follow company backup and recovery procedures

**Sharing:**

1. **Verify Recipients:**
   - Always verify the identity of people requesting sensitive information
   - Confirm they have a legitimate need for the data
   - Check if they have proper authorisation

2. **Use Secure Channels:**
   - Use encrypted communication channels
   - Use company-approved file-sharing methods
   - Avoid sending sensitive data via unencrypted email

3. **Minimise Data Shared:**

### What are some **common mistakes** that lead to data privacy issues, and how can they be avoided?

**Common Mistakes:**

1. **Weak Access Controls:**
   - **Mistake:** Using weak passwords, sharing credentials, not enabling two-factor authentication
   - **Avoid:** Use strong, unique passwords; never share credentials; enable 2FA everywhere possible

2. **Unsecured Storage:**
   - **Mistake:** Storing sensitive data on personal devices, unencrypted storage, or public cloud accounts
   - **Avoid:** Use company-approved, encrypted storage solutions; follow data storage policies

3. **Inappropriate Sharing:**
   - **Mistake:** Sharing sensitive data via unencrypted email, public channels, or with unauthorised people
   - **Avoid:** Use secure channels; verify recipients; only share with authorised personnel

4. **Lack of Data Minimisation:**
   - **Mistake:** Collecting or retaining more data than necessary, keeping data longer than needed
   - **Avoid:** Only collect necessary data; delete data when no longer needed; follow retention policies

5. **Phishing and Social Engineering:**
   - **Mistake:** Falling for phishing emails, sharing credentials, or providing information to unauthorised people
   - **Avoid:** Verify sender identity; be cautious of suspicious requests; never share credentials via email

6. **Unlocked Devices:**
   - **Mistake:** Leaving devices unlocked, unattended, or accessible to others
   - **Avoid:** Lock screens when away; use screen locks; secure physical devices

7. **Insufficient Training:**
   - **Mistake:** Not understanding privacy policies, regulations, or best practices
   - **Avoid:** Stay informed about privacy regulations; attend training; ask questions

8. **Delayed Incident Response:**
   - **Mistake:** Not reporting breaches immediately, trying to fix issues alone, or covering up mistakes
   - **Avoid:** Report incidents immediately; involve appropriate team members; don't delay

9. **Inadequate Documentation:**
   - **Mistake:** Not documenting data access, sharing, or disposal; lack of audit trails
   - **Avoid:** Keep records of data handling; document processes; maintain audit trails

10. **Ignoring User Rights:**
    - **Mistake:** Not responding to user requests for access, deletion, or correction
    - **Avoid:** Understand user rights under GDPR; respond promptly to requests; follow procedures

**Prevention Strategies:**

1. **Regular Training:**
   - Attend privacy and security training sessions
   - Stay updated on regulations and best practices
   - Learn from case studies and examples

2. **Security Awareness:**
   - Be vigilant about phishing and social engineering
   - Question suspicious requests
   - Verify identities before sharing sensitive information

3. **Use Security Tools:**
   - Enable security features (2FA, encryption, screen locks)
   - Use company-approved security tools
   - Keep software and systems updated

4. **Follow Policies:**
   - Read and understand company privacy policies
   - Follow data handling procedures
   - Ask questions when unsure

5. **Regular Reviews:**
   - Periodically review data access and usage
   - Remove unnecessary access
   - Audit data storage and sharing practices

**Practice: "Verify Before Sharing" Protocol**

I will adopt a "Verify Before Sharing" protocol for all data sharing activities. This means:

1. **Before Sharing Any Data:**

   - Confirm they have a legitimate need and proper authorisation
   - Check if the minimum necessary data can be shared instead of full datasets
   - Use secure, encrypted channels for sharing

2. **Documentation:**
   - Keep a brief log of what data was shared, with whom, when, and why
   - Note the purpose and authorisation for sharing
   - This creates an audit trail and helps identify patterns

3. **Regular Review:**
   - Weekly review of data sharing activities
   - Identify any unnecessary sharing
   - Revoke access when no longer needed

**Why This Helps:**
- Prevents unauthorised access to sensitive data
- Ensures compliance with data minimisation principles
- Creates accountability and audit trails
- Reduces risk of accidental disclosure
- Builds good privacy habits
