### What is Agile, and how does it differ from traditional project management?

Agile is an iterative, incremental approach to delivering value through short feedback cycles, continuous learning, and adaptable plans. Traditional project management (often ‚Äúwaterfall‚Äù) tends to be plan-driven, with fixed scope, sequential phases, and change managed through formal approvals. Agile prioritises responsiveness to change and frequent delivery over comprehensive upfront planning and rigid phase gates.

### Core values and principles of Agile

#### Values

- Individuals and interactions over processes and tools
- Working software over comprehensive documentation
- Customer collaboration over contract negotiation
- Responding to change over following a plan

#### Principles

- Satisfy the customer through early and continuous delivery
- Welcome changing requirements, even late in development
- Deliver working software frequently
- Business and developers collaborate daily
- Build around motivated individuals and trust them
- Prefer face-to-face communication
- Working software is the primary measure of progress
- Maintain a sustainable pace
- Continuous attention to technical excellence and good design
- Simplicity is essential
- Best solutions emerge from self-organising teams
- Regularly reflect and adjust behaviour

### Biggest benefits and challenges of Agile

#### Benefits

- Faster feedback and higher adaptability
- Earlier value delivery and visibility
- Stronger collaboration and ownership

#### Challenges

- Requires disciplined communication and transparency
- Scope can drift without clear prioritisation
- Teams must balance speed with technical quality
